# Scoring Rubric

Score each role on a 0-100 scale. Use judgment, but keep the weighting stable.

## Weights

- 0-30: Background match with `USER.md`
- 0-25: Alignment with prioritized role families in `PREFERENCES.md`
- 0-15: Skill-signal overlap
- 0-15: Work model and location fit
- 0-10: Seniority fit
- 0-5: Research or domain relevance bonus

## Interpretation

- 85-100: Strong shortlist candidate
- 70-84: Good candidate worth showing
- 55-69: Borderline candidate, include only if the portal is sparse
- Below 55: Exclude unless the user explicitly wants stretch options

## Scoring Guidance

Give high background-match scores when the posting directly matches the user's demonstrated stack, domain, or research history in `USER.md`.

Give high role-family scores when the title and responsibilities align with the role families or career tracks prioritized in `PREFERENCES.md`.

Give high skill-signal scores when the posting names technologies or methods already supported by the user's profile or stated preferences.

Give high work-model and location scores when the posting aligns with the user's stated constraints and preferences in `PREFERENCES.md`. Reduce or remove credit when the posting conflicts with those constraints.

Reduce seniority scores when the role is materially above or below the user's target level as described in `PREFERENCES.md`, unless the overall fit is unusually strong.

Use the research or domain bonus only when the posting values background areas that are both supported by `USER.md` and explicitly desirable under `PREFERENCES.md`.

## Tie-Breakers

Break ties in this order:

1. Better work-model fit
2. Better evidence of direct skill overlap
3. Lower obvious seniority risk
4. Clearer day-to-day alignment with the user's likely interests
