# Job Search Preferences

## Employment
- Work Type: Full-time

## Work Model Priority
1. Fully remote roles anywhere in the United States
2. Hybrid roles in the San Francisco Bay Area only if they are especially strong matches
3. Onsite roles should generally be excluded

## Seniority Priority
- Primary target: Entry/Grad, Junior, Mid
- Stretch target: Senior only when the role is an unusually strong match
- Avoid principal/staff/architect roles unless the role is highly specialised and matches my background extremely closely
- For research-oriented roles, include positions that are implementation-heavy, theory-heavy, or mixed research/engineering, even if the title is slightly more senior, when the fit is strong

## Geographic Logic
- Do not prioritise Bay Area location over a fully remote U.S. role
- Bay Area is a secondary preference for strong hybrid opportunities, not a primary filter
- National remote roles should be searched first across all portals

## Role Families To Prioritise
- Software Engineer / Software Engineer II / Product Engineer
- Systems Software Engineer
- Cloud Platform / Infrastructure Engineer
- Developer Tools / Internal Tools / Automation Tools
- Research Tools / Technical Staff Engineering
- Full-Stack Engineering
- Backend / Platform Engineering
- AI Infrastructure / Distributed Systems / HPC / Networking
- Applied AI Engineering
- Enterprise Infrastructure
- Research Engineer / Research Software Engineer
- Applied Research Engineering
- Research Infrastructure / Experiment Infrastructure
- Research Scientist / Applied Scientist / Member of Technical Staff
- Computational Biology / Bioinformatics Engineering
- Scientific Computing / HPC Research Systems
- Theoretical or algorithmic research roles where my background is relevant
- Selected FPGA / ASIC / silicon-adjacent roles when my background is unusually strong

## Skill Signals To Prioritise
- TypeScript, React, Next.js
- Python, C, C++
- AWS, Docker, Kubernetes, backend APIs, distributed systems
- HPC, MPI, CUDA, high-performance networking, InfiniBand, RoCE
- AI tooling, evaluation systems, agentic workflows, internal platforms
- Research tooling, experiment infrastructure, benchmarking, simulation, model evaluation, scientific workflows
- Computational biology, bioinformatics, scientific computing, data analysis pipelines where explicitly relevant
- Theoretical research ability, algorithm design, mathematical modelling, experimental design, and publication record where explicitly relevant
- FPGA, RTL, SystemVerilog, ASIC, RISC-V where explicitly relevant
- PhD-level research experience, publications, patents, or formal experimental work when the role values them

## Search Rules
- Search for explicit remote roles first
- Only after exhausting strong remote options, include Bay Area hybrid roles
- Search by both title and skill overlap, not title alone
- Include broad titles such as “Software Engineer”, “Software Engineer II”, or “Systems Engineer” if the description matches my skills
- Include research-oriented titles such as “Research Engineer”, “Research Software Engineer”, “Applied Research Engineer”, “Research Infrastructure Engineer”, “Research Scientist”, and “Applied Scientist” when the subject matter fits my background
- Prioritise research roles that involve building tools, systems, infrastructure, experiments, evaluations, simulations, theory, algorithms, modelling, or production-quality prototypes
- Do not over-prioritise prestige teams, frontier AI branding, or highly research-adjacent titles if a more practical remote engineering role is a better fit
- Do not assume a role is remote or hybrid unless the advert states it explicitly
- Prefer roles that are likely to yield interviews, not just roles with the highest theoretical technical overlap
- Do not restrict searches to conventional product or platform engineering titles only; include research-facing roles where my PhD and publications are relevant

## Ranking Logic
Rank opportunities in this order:
1. Explicitly remote status
2. Level fit
3. Skill overlap with my recent work
4. Match to my research background where relevant
5. Likelihood of interview
6. Compensation and strategic upside

## What Counts As Strong Overlap
A role should be prioritised if it substantially matches one or more of these profiles:
- Full-stack/product/tooling engineer using TypeScript/React/Next.js + Python/backend systems
- Cloud/platform/infrastructure engineer working on large-scale services, tooling, or developer platforms
- Distributed systems / HPC / AI infrastructure engineer working on performance, networking, orchestration, or scalability
- Research tools / experiment tooling / internal productivity systems
- Research engineer building evaluation systems, simulations, benchmarking frameworks, experiment infrastructure, or scientific software
- Research scientist or applied scientist role involving algorithms, modelling, AI/ML methods, computational methods, or other theory-heavy research relevant to my background
- Computational biology / bioinformatics / scientific computing role where my research background is directly relevant
- Silicon / FPGA / ASIC engineering roles where my hardware background is directly relevant

## Research-Oriented Role Guidance
- Prioritise research-oriented roles that value a PhD, publications, patents, experimentation, theory, or cross-disciplinary technical work
- Prefer roles where the deliverables may include software, systems, infrastructure, tooling, experiments, applied technical prototypes, theoretical advances, algorithms, models, or new methods
- Include roles that sit between engineering and research, as well as roles that are primarily research-driven, especially in AI, scientific computing, computational biology, hardware-accelerated computing, and evaluation/tooling
- Include theoretical, algorithmic, and scientist-led roles when the topic area matches my background well
- Deprioritise only those research roles that depend mainly on a very specific publication niche or domain speciality that I do not substantially match

## Exclusions
- Fully onsite roles
- Roles where office-heavy hybrid is the default unless the fit is exceptional
- Roles that are clearly too senior in scope unless they are unusually aligned
- Roles that are mostly managerial, recruiter-facing, solutions-architect, sales, or customer-success oriented

## Search Behaviour For Future Shortlists
When generating job matches:
- Show remote roles first
- Treat hybrid Bay Area roles as secondary
- Prefer practical, interviewable matches over aspirational prestige picks
- Use the actual job description and work model as the source of truth
- Optimise for finding roles similar to my existing applications in cloud platform, tools, infrastructure, AI systems, HPC, full-stack engineering, and research-oriented work
- Account for both my engineering background and my PhD/research profile when identifying strong matches
- Include both engineering-heavy and theory-heavy research roles where the underlying domain is a strong match
